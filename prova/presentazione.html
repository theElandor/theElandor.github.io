Multi-agent systems

Introduction

What are multi-agent systems?

They are artificial systems, sometimes related to nature-inspired computing, composed of multiple interacting intelligent agents in a dynamic environment.

Agents (1/2)

Some several important characteristics:

  • Autonomy (decentralization)
  • Reactivness
  • Proactivity
  • Comunication skills

Agents (2/2)

Two more categories of agents…

  • Passive
  • Active

Environment

Sometimes plays an active role, by executing pressure on the agents. In that case, agents must be able to resist and sometimes adapt.
No agent has a full global view of the world!

Why multi agent systems?

In computer science, sometimes it is easier (or needed) to think of problems in a distributed way:

  • Swarms of drones
  • Smart cities
  • Self-driving and transportation
  • And much more…

Swarm Intelligence

Introduction (1/3)

What is swarm intelligence?
Let's give a formal definition…

It is the collective behavior of decentralized, self-organized systems.

Introduction(2/3)

In other words, it is a feature of some multi-agent systems that can perform tasks in a """smart""" way.

Introduction (3/3)

It is often related to natural phenomena (ant foraging, bird flocking…). Let's take a look at some different examples…

Agents

  1. Initially generated at the center of the nest.
  2. Look for food, trying to follow feromone gradient.
  3. Once food is found, turn around and go back following the smell of the nest.
  4. While going back, release feromone.

And remember: each ant moves and acts independently!

Environment

The environment has an active role:

  1. The nest (positioned at the center) has a gradient of smell that ants can perceive;
  2. The feromone released by ants spreads and eventually evaporates;

Ants gradually consume every food pile, following the shotest path for each one of them!

The feedback mechanism is what coordinates the collective movement

Feedback (1/2)

  • Positive feedback: the actions of an agent cause more of that action to occur in other agents;
  • Negative feedback the actions of an agent cause less of that action to occur in other agents;

Feedback (2/2)

Every ant modifies the environment around it when returning to the nest, by releasing feromone. \[\Downarrow\] More and more ants will follow that same path. \[\Downarrow\] Positive feedback

Conclusions

Randomness is actually a good thing

Theese models are often based on random decisions. \[\Downarrow\] The system is always changing. \[\Downarrow\] Adaptability

A large number of agents is needed

A model needs many agents to reach self-organization. \[\Downarrow\] Theese systems are noise-resistant: even if an agent dies, the model is not affected significantly.
